A Landmark Moment for Couples in Greece

The legal foundation could not be clearer. Greece legalised same-sex civil marriage in February 2024, becoming the first majority Orthodox Christian country in the world to do so. The law grants same-sex couples the full right to marry, and to adopt, placing Greece among the European nations that recognise complete marriage equality.

For couples, this means a wedding in Santorini is no longer only symbolic. You can now marry legally on the island, with the same standing as any other couple. It is a profound and joyful change, and it has opened Santorini's cliffs, sunsets and caldera views to a whole new chapter of celebrations.

Practical Planning Considerations

A smooth same-sex wedding in Santorini comes down to the same fundamentals that make any destination wedding succeed, handled with care.

Decide early whether you want a legally binding civil ceremony or a symbolic one, as this shapes your documentation and timeline. If you choose a legal ceremony, the necessary paperwork, including certificates, apostilles and translations, is best managed by your planning team so you can focus on the celebration. Think too about your guest list and logistics: Santorini's cliffside villages are stunning but can involve steps and transfers, so venue choice and transport matter. And book early, particularly for peak season and sunset slots, which are reserved well in advance.
